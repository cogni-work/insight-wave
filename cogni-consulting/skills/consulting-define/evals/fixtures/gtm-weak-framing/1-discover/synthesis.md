# Discovery Synthesis — HealthBridge GTM Expansion

**Engagement**: HealthBridge GTM Expansion (HealthBridge Inc.)
**Vision Class**: gtm-roadmap
**Methods**: Desk Research, Competitive Analysis
**Date**: 2026-03-19

## Themes

### 1. Market is Growing
The mid-market healthcare SaaS market is projected to grow significantly over the next several years. Digital health spending is increasing across DACH and US markets. Multiple analysts expect continued growth.
- **Source**: Desk Research
- **So what**: There is an opportunity to enter a growing market.

### 2. Competition Exists but is Fragmented
Several competitors serve the mid-market healthcare space. Most are point solutions (scheduling, billing, EHR) rather than integrated platforms. No single dominant player exists in the 50-500 staff segment.
- **Source**: Competitive Analysis
- **So what**: The fragmented competitive landscape may offer a window for an integrated platform.

### 3. Regulatory Requirements are Significant
HIPAA compliance (US) and GDPR-health provisions (EU) create barriers to entry. Certification processes take 6-12 months. Ongoing compliance maintenance is resource-intensive.
- **Source**: Desk Research
- **So what**: Regulatory readiness will take time and investment.

### 4. Sales Team Lacks Healthcare Experience
The current sales team has B2B SaaS experience but no vertical healthcare knowledge. Healthcare buyers have specific evaluation criteria and procurement processes that differ from general B2B.
- **Source**: Desk Research (industry reports on healthcare procurement)
- **So what**: Sales enablement and potentially healthcare-specific hiring will be needed.

### 5. Platform Architecture May Need Adaptation
The existing platform was built for general practice management. Mid-market healthcare workflows (multi-department scheduling, compliance reporting, EHR integration) may require architectural changes.
- **Source**: Desk Research
- **So what**: Technical feasibility assessment is needed before committing to the expansion.

## Surprises

1. **No real surprises emerged** — the desk research largely confirmed the initial hypothesis that mid-market healthcare is an attractive but complex market to enter.

## Tensions

1. **Speed vs. Compliance**: The client wants to enter the market quickly, but regulatory certification takes 6-12 months minimum.

## Readiness for Define

**Assessment**: Marginally ready. The evidence base is limited to desk research and competitive analysis — no stakeholder mapping, customer journey analysis, or trend scouting was performed. Themes are broad rather than specific to HealthBridge's situation. No primary research or direct healthcare stakeholder input was gathered.

**Known gaps**: No customer/user perspective (no stakeholder mapping or customer journey analysis). No trend analysis to contextualize market timing. Competitive analysis is surface-level — feature-by-feature comparison not performed. All themes are confirmatory — no genuine surprises or challenging findings.
