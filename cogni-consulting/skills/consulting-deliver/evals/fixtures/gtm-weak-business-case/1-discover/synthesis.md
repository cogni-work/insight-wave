# Discovery Synthesis

## Key Findings

### Market Landscape
- Remote patient monitoring (RPM) market estimated at €4.2B in Europe by 2028 (Grand View Research)
- Germany DiGA registry: 62 approved apps as of Q1 2026, 8 in RPM category, reimbursement €200-800/patient/quarter
- UK NHS Long Term Plan includes digital health expansion, but DTAC compliance pathway reformed in 2025 — new requirements not yet fully defined
- Competitor landscape fragmented: 12+ RPM platforms in DACH, none with dominant share in statutory health insurance channel

### Regulatory Environment
- DiGA approval timeline: 12-18 months from submission (BfArM fast-track available for certain categories)
- CE marking (MDR Class IIa) required before DiGA submission — HealthBridge has CE marking in progress, expected Q2 2026
- UK DTAC: post-2025 reform has introduced uncertainty; several digital health companies have paused UK market entry

### Competitive Position
- HealthBridge differentiator: integrated clinical decision support (AI-driven) alongside monitoring — most competitors offer monitoring only
- Weakness: no clinical evidence from RCTs; competitor Kaia Health has published 3 peer-reviewed studies
- Pricing gap: HealthBridge targets €600/patient/quarter, market average is €350-450

### Surprises
- Discovery was desk research and competitive analysis only — no stakeholder interviews with NHS procurement or GKV Krankenkassen were conducted
- The €4.2B market size figure is for all of European RPM, not the addressable segments HealthBridge targets
- No customer validation data exists — the product-market fit assumption is based on founder conviction and one informal conversation with a Krankenkasse innovation manager

## Tensions
- Runway (18 months) vs. DiGA timeline (12-18 months for approval alone) — if approval takes the full 18 months, there is zero sales time before funding runs out
- Premium pricing (€600) vs. market average (€350-450) — requires strong clinical evidence that doesn't exist yet
- Partner-led distribution assumed but no distributor LOI or even formal conversations have occurred
