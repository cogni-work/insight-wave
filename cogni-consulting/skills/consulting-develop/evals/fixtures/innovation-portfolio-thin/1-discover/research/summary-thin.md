# Desk Research Summary — InnoVentures Portfolio Strategy

**Research scope**: Innovation portfolio management practices in mid-size industrial automation firms
**Status**: Thin results — only 2 substantive findings. Broader research scope or alternative sources recommended.

---

## Finding 1: Portfolio Sprawl is Industry-Wide

McKinsey's 2025 Innovation Benchmark Report and BCG's "Managing the Innovation Portfolio" (2024) both find that 60-70% of mid-size industrial firms (30-100 R&D headcount) struggle with portfolio proliferation. Common pattern: projects are easy to start but hard to kill, leading to resource dilution across too many bets. The firms that outperform tend to have explicit kill criteria and quarterly portfolio reviews.

**Relevance**: InnoVentures fits the pattern precisely — 12 projects across 42 engineers is a 3.5 engineer-per-project average, which both reports flag as below the threshold for meaningful progress.

## Finding 2: Evaluation Frameworks That Stick

A Harvard Business Review article (2025) on "Building Innovation Evaluation Muscle" argues that the primary failure mode of portfolio prioritization is treating it as a one-time exercise. Companies that build lasting capability share three traits: (a) they use a common evaluation language across business units, (b) they review quarterly rather than annually, and (c) they give project leads visibility into portfolio-level decisions so they understand why their project was deprioritized.

**Relevance**: Directly supports the "capability vs. ranking" tension identified in stakeholder interviews. The risk for InnoVentures is producing a ranking that decays within 6 months.
