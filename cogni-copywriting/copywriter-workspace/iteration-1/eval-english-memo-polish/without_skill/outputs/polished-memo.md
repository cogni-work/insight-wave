# Cloud Migration Update

## Bottom Line

The cloud migration is 75% complete and ahead of schedule, delivering measurable performance and cost improvements. To maintain momentum, we need executive approval of $450K in additional security budget and a decision on the legacy application migration approach by end of Q2.

## Key Results

- **75% of workloads migrated to AWS**, ahead of the original timeline.
- **Application performance has improved significantly**, with faster response times across all services.
- **Infrastructure costs have decreased** compared to the prior on-premise environment.

## Open Challenges

### Legacy Applications
Approximately 25% of workloads remain on legacy systems built on older technology that is incompatible with cloud-native architectures. The team is evaluating two options:

1. **Containerization** of existing applications.
2. **Selective rewriting** of the most problematic systems.

Both options will require more time and resources than originally scoped.

### Security Gaps
An internal review has identified gaps in cloud monitoring and incident response capabilities. Closing these gaps requires:

- Deployment of additional security tooling.
- Hiring of two security engineers.
- **Estimated cost: $450K.**

## Decisions Needed

| Decision | Owner | Deadline |
|---|---|---|
| Approve $450K security budget | Executive Team | Immediate |
| Finalize legacy application migration approach | Project Team | End of Q2 |
| Schedule stakeholder review to align on revised timeline | Project Lead | Within 2 weeks |

## Outlook

The project is on a strong trajectory. Timely decisions on security funding and the legacy migration strategy will keep the program on track for completion.
