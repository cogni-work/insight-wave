# The Operations Portfolio, Reframed

*How three buyer jobs reorganize an IT operations capability set*

## Job Landscape

Your portfolio lists six products. Your buyer has three jobs. Operations leaders do not wake up wanting "an observability suite" — they wake up needing to keep services running, prove resilience to auditors, and free engineers from firefighting [P1-1](https://www.gartner.com/aiops-market-guide-2025).

The three jobs that organize everything else: reduce unplanned downtime below 2%, demonstrate audit-ready resilience across regulated workloads, and reclaim engineering hours lost to manual triage. Each is a verb-phrase the buyer would actually say out loud [P1-2](https://www.pagerduty.com/state-of-digital-ops-2025).

## Friction Map

Reducing unplanned downtime is blocked by fragmented telemetry: 47 incompatible data formats and roughly 340 false positives per week bury the signals that matter. The cost of inaction runs to an estimated $5.6 million annually in direct downtime [P2-1](https://www.idc.com/enterprise-ai-adoption-tracker).

Proving resilience is blocked by evidence sprawl. Auditors ask for continuous control data; teams assemble it by hand across disconnected tools, spending 12 hours per audit cycle on correlation alone [P2-2](https://www.eba.europa.eu/digital-resilience-guidelines).

## Portfolio Map

For the job of reducing unplanned downtime: a sensor-fusion layer ingests all 47 formats into one asset-health model. You detect failure patterns up to 72 hours before breakdown, cutting unplanned downtime by 63% [P3-1](https://www.aiops-vendor.com/platform-overview).

For the job of proving resilience: a continuous-evidence module maps live signals to control frameworks. You hand auditors a standing dashboard instead of a manual binder, and the 12-hour correlation cycle collapses to minutes [P3-2](https://www.aiops-vendor.com/customer-results).

## Invitation

Start with your highest-friction job. A six-week scoped engagement instruments one job end-to-end and returns a measured friction-reduction baseline you can take to your board.

When the baseline lands, `/why-change` tailors the deal-specific case for the buyer in the room.
