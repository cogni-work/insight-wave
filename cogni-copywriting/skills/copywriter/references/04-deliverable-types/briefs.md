# Professional Briefs

## Quick Reference

**Purpose:** Concise decision support for executives
**Length:** 1-3 pages (500-1200 words)
**Formality:** Medium-high
**Audience:** Executives, decision-makers, board members
**Best frameworks:** BLUF (decision briefs), Pyramid (strategic briefs), SCQA (problem-solving briefs)
**Reading time:** 2-5 minutes maximum
**Core requirement:** Every brief must answer "What should we do?" not just "What happened?"

## When to Write a Brief (Not Something Else)

Choose the right deliverable by matching the communication need:

| Need | Deliverable | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| Recommend a decision with supporting analysis | **Brief** | Structured for evaluation and action |
| Summarize a longer document for leadership | Executive summary | Condenses existing content |
| Communicate policy or update to a team | Memo | Internal, informational |
| Request a single action from one person | Email | Direct, conversational |
| Present comprehensive research or findings | Report | Detailed, multi-section |
| Sell an idea with persuasive narrative | Proposal | Audience needs convincing |

**Rule of thumb:** If the reader must decide or approve something based on structured analysis, write a brief.

## Standard Structure

Every brief follows a three-part arc: state the answer, prove the answer, specify the next action.

### Decision Brief (BLUF Framework)

Use when an executive must approve or reject a specific action.

```markdown
# [Decision Required]: [Topic]

**BLUF:** Recommend [specific decision] by [date] to [achieve measurable outcome].

## Context
[2-3 sentences of essential background. What changed? Why now?]

## Analysis

### Option A: [Name]
- **Pros:** [concrete benefits with numbers]
- **Cons:** [concrete risks with numbers]
- **Cost:** [dollar amount and timeline]

### Option B: [Name]
- **Pros:** [concrete benefits with numbers]
- **Cons:** [concrete risks with numbers]
- **Cost:** [dollar amount and timeline]

### Option C: Do Nothing
- **Consequence:** [what happens if no action taken, quantified]

## Recommendation
[Restate recommendation with 1-2 sentences of rationale linking to the strongest evidence above.]

## Next Steps
1. [Action] by [date] by [person/team]
2. [Action] by [date] by [person/team]
3. [Action] by [date] by [person/team]
```

### Strategic Brief (Pyramid Framework)

Use when presenting a recommendation backed by multiple lines of evidence.

```markdown
# [Topic] Strategic Brief

## Recommendation
[Main conclusion/recommendation in 2-3 sentences with quantified impact.]

## Supporting Arguments

### 1. [First Argument Headline]
[3-5 evidence points supporting this argument. Use metrics.]

### 2. [Second Argument Headline]
[3-5 evidence points supporting this argument. Use metrics.]

### 3. [Third Argument Headline]
[3-5 evidence points supporting this argument. Use metrics.]

## Financial Analysis
[Table comparing costs, timelines, and returns across options.]

## Next Steps
[Numbered list of actions with owners and deadlines.]
```

### Problem-Solving Brief (SCQA Framework)

Use when the audience needs to understand a problem before the recommendation makes sense.

```markdown
# [Topic]: Assessment and Recommendation

## Situation
[Current state. Objective facts the reader already knows or accepts. 2-3 sentences.]

## Complication
[What changed or went wrong. Why the current state is unsustainable. Include metrics showing severity.]

## Question
[The strategic question this brief answers. Frame as "Should we X or Y?" or "How do we address Z?"]

## Answer
[Clear recommendation with quantified expected outcome.]

## Supporting Analysis
[Evidence, options considered, financial impact.]

## Next Steps
[Actions, owners, deadlines.]
```

## Framework Integration Examples

### Decision Brief Example (BLUF)

```markdown
# Decision Required: Customer Data Platform Vendor Selection

BLUF: Approve Segment ($180K/year) as our customer data platform by March 1
to unify our fragmented customer data and enable $2.1M in projected
cross-sell revenue by Q4.

## Context
We currently operate five disconnected customer databases across sales,
marketing, support, product, and billing. This fragmentation caused $340K
in duplicate outreach costs last year and prevents cross-sell targeting.

## Analysis

### Option A: Segment ($180K/year)
- Pros: Integrates with our existing stack (Salesforce, HubSpot, Zendesk),
  30-day deployment, 4.7/5 G2 rating for mid-market
- Cons: Annual contract required, limited real-time processing
- Timeline: Live by April 1

### Option B: mParticle ($260K/year)
- Pros: Superior real-time processing, stronger enterprise features
- Cons: 90-day deployment, requires dedicated engineer, 45% more expensive
- Timeline: Live by June 1

### Option C: Do Nothing
- Consequence: Continue losing $340K/year in duplicate costs, miss $2.1M
  cross-sell opportunity, fall further behind competitors who launched
  unified profiles in 2025

## Recommendation
Approve Segment. It meets 90% of our requirements at 30% lower cost
than mParticle, with a deployment timeline that captures Q3 cross-sell
opportunities. Real-time processing gaps are addressable in Year 2.

## Next Steps
1. Finance approves PO by March 1
2. Engineering begins integration sprint March 3
3. Marketing prepares cross-sell campaigns for April launch
4. First unified customer profiles available April 1
```

### Strategic Brief Example (Pyramid)

```markdown
# Employee Retention Strategy

## Recommendation
Invest $400K in a three-part retention program (compensation adjustment,
career pathing, flexible work policy) to reduce attrition from 28% to
15% within 12 months, saving an estimated $1.8M in annual replacement costs.

## Supporting Arguments

### 1. Current Attrition Is Financially Unsustainable
- 28% annual attrition costs $2.4M/year in recruiting and onboarding
- Average time-to-productivity for replacements: 6.2 months
- Knowledge loss from departing senior engineers caused 3 project delays
  in Q4

### 2. Exit Data Points to Three Fixable Root Causes
- 62% cited below-market compensation (we pay 12% below median)
- 48% cited lack of career growth (no promotion framework exists)
- 41% cited inflexible work policy (competitors offer full remote)
- These three causes account for 85% of voluntary departures

### 3. Targeted Interventions Show Proven ROI
- Industry data: 10% compensation adjustment reduces attrition by 20-25%
- Companies with formal career pathing report 34% higher retention
  (LinkedIn Workplace Learning Report)
- Our Q3 remote-work pilot: zero attrition among 50 participants vs. 31%
  in office-only cohort

## Financial Analysis

| Investment | Annual Cost | Expected Impact | ROI |
|-----------|------------|-----------------|-----|
| Compensation adjustment (10%) | $280K | 20-25% attrition reduction | 3.4x |
| Career pathing program | $80K | 15-20% attrition reduction | 7.5x |
| Flexible work policy | $40K (tooling) | 10-15% attrition reduction | 15x |
| **Total** | **$400K** | **Target: 28% to 15%** | **4.5x** |

## Next Steps
1. HR presents compensation benchmarks to CFO by March 15
2. L&D drafts career framework v1 by April 1
3. IT provisions remote collaboration tools by April 15
4. Policy rollout begins May 1
```

### Problem-Solving Brief Example (SCQA)

```markdown
# Website Performance: Assessment and Recommendation

## Situation
Our e-commerce site processes 2.3M monthly visits and generates $18M
in annual revenue. Page load time has been a stable 2.1 seconds for
the past two years.

## Complication
Since the January platform update, average page load increased to 4.8
seconds. Conversion rate dropped from 3.2% to 1.9%, costing approximately
$180K/month in lost revenue. Mobile users (61% of traffic) are most
affected, with 38% bounce rate versus 15% pre-update.

## Question
Should we roll back the platform update, optimize the current build,
or migrate to a new architecture?

## Answer
Optimize the current build ($45K, 3-week sprint) to restore sub-2-second
load times. This preserves the update's security and feature benefits
while addressing the performance regression.

## Supporting Analysis
- Rollback removes critical security patches and 4 revenue features
- Migration ($300K, 6 months) is the right long-term move but does not
  address the immediate $180K/month revenue loss
- Performance audit identified 3 specific bottlenecks: unoptimized images
  (40% of load time), render-blocking scripts (30%), database query
  inefficiency (20%)
- Optimization sprint addresses all three with proven techniques

## Next Steps
1. Engineering begins optimization sprint February 28
2. Staged rollout to 25% of traffic by March 7 to validate improvements
3. Full deployment by March 14
4. Begin architecture migration planning for Q3 (separate brief to follow)
```

## Writing Rules for Briefs

### Lead With the Answer

The first substantive sentence of every brief must state the recommendation or conclusion. Executives read top-down and may stop at any point.

**Before (buries the lead):**
```
Over the past quarter, we have been evaluating several customer data
platforms to address our ongoing data fragmentation challenges. After
extensive analysis of market options and internal requirements gathering
with stakeholders across multiple departments, our team has identified
key criteria...
```

**After (answer first):**
```
BLUF: Approve Segment ($180K/year) as our customer data platform by
March 1 to unify fragmented customer data and enable $2.1M in projected
cross-sell revenue.
```

### Quantify Everything

Briefs persuade through evidence. Replace qualitative language with specific metrics.

| Weak (qualitative) | Strong (quantified) |
|----|----|
| "Significant cost savings" | "$450K annual savings (35% reduction)" |
| "Improved customer satisfaction" | "NPS increased from 32 to 58" |
| "Faster response times" | "Response time reduced from 11 min to 45 sec" |
| "Growing market opportunity" | "$4.2M addressable market, 23% YoY growth" |
| "Several vendors evaluated" | "Evaluated 5 vendors across 12 criteria" |
| "Recently increased" | "Increased 40% since Q2 2025" |

### Make Every Sentence Earn Its Place

Briefs have a hard length constraint. Apply these compression tests:

1. **Does this sentence help the reader decide?** If not, cut it.
2. **Is this background the reader already knows?** If so, cut it.
3. **Can this paragraph become a bullet point?** If so, compress it.
4. **Can this prose become a table?** If comparing options, use a table.

**Before (68 words):**
```
We have been working with our vendor partners over the past several months
to negotiate more favorable pricing terms for our Q4 marketing campaign.
After extensive discussions and multiple rounds of negotiation, we have
successfully secured rates that represent a meaningful discount compared to
what we paid in Q3, which should result in better overall return on our
marketing investment.
```

**After (22 words):**
```
Negotiated Q4 vendor rates 18% below Q3 pricing. Expected ROI improvement:
$200K revenue on $50K spend (4x return).
```

### Present Options Fairly, Then Recommend Clearly

Show the reader you considered alternatives. A recommendation without visible alternatives looks like advocacy, not analysis.

**Pattern for option comparison:**

| Criterion | Option A | Option B | Do Nothing |
|-----------|----------|----------|------------|
| Cost | $X | $Y | $Z (hidden cost) |
| Timeline | N weeks | N months | N/A |
| Risk | Low/Med/High | Low/Med/High | [quantified risk] |
| Expected outcome | [metric] | [metric] | [metric] |

Always include a "Do Nothing" option to make the cost of inaction visible.

### Use Parallel Structure

Within any list, table, or set of options, maintain consistent grammatical structure. This aids scanning speed and reduces cognitive load.

**Before (inconsistent structure):**
```
- Digital ads will cost $30K and have shown a 15% conversion rate
- $15K is the budget for content production
- We also need analytics, which runs about $5K
```

**After (parallel structure):**
```
- Digital ads: $30K (15% conversion rate)
- Content production: $15K (video + blog series)
- Analytics tools: $5K (ROI tracking)
```

## Common Brief Types

### Decision Brief
- Opens with specific recommendation
- Presents 2-3 options with pros/cons/costs
- Includes "Do Nothing" option with consequences
- Ends with clear approval request and deadline
- **Best framework:** BLUF

### Strategic Brief
- Opens with strategic recommendation
- Supports with 3 MECE arguments (Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive)
- Each argument backed by 3-5 evidence points
- Includes financial analysis table
- **Best framework:** Pyramid

### Situation Brief
- Opens with current state assessment
- Identifies root causes of problem
- Proposes solution with implementation plan
- **Best framework:** SCQA

### Competitive Brief
- Opens with competitive position summary
- Analyzes 2-4 competitor moves
- Identifies threats and opportunities
- Recommends strategic response
- **Best framework:** Pyramid

### Investment Brief
- Opens with investment recommendation and expected return
- Presents financial model (costs, revenue, timeline to break-even)
- Addresses risk factors and mitigation
- Includes sensitivity analysis for key assumptions
- **Best framework:** Pyramid

## Tone and Voice

### Authoritative but Balanced
Briefs must sound confident without being dismissive of alternatives.

**Good:** "Recommend Segment based on superior integration capabilities and 30% cost advantage."
**Too tentative:** "It might be worth considering whether Segment could potentially be a good fit."
**Too aggressive:** "Segment is clearly the only viable option and any other choice would be a mistake."

### Objective and Evidence-Based
Let data carry the argument. Avoid emotional or subjective language.

**Good:** "Attrition increased from 18% to 28%, costing $2.4M in replacement expenses."
**Bad:** "We are hemorrhaging talent at an alarming and unprecedented rate."

### Action-Oriented
Use active voice and direct verbs. The brief should convey forward momentum.

**Good:** "Approve the $280K investment by November 15 to meet the Q1 deployment window."
**Bad:** "It is recommended that consideration be given to the approval of the investment."

## Visual Elements

**Target:** 1-3 visual elements per brief. Use visuals to compress information, not to decorate.

### When to Use Tables
- Comparing options across multiple criteria
- Presenting financial analysis (costs, timelines, returns)
- Showing before/after metrics

### When to Use Bullet Points
- Listing evidence points under an argument
- Enumerating next steps
- Presenting pros and cons

### When to Use Bold
- Key metrics within prose ("attrition increased to **28%**")
- Option names in analysis sections
- Deadlines and action owners in next steps

**See also:** [visual-elements.md](../03-formatting-standards/visual-elements.md)

## Quality Checklist

Before delivering:

- [ ] Recommendation stated in first 1-3 sentences
- [ ] Every claim backed by a specific metric or evidence point
- [ ] Options compared fairly (including "Do Nothing")
- [ ] Financial impact quantified (cost, savings, ROI, break-even)
- [ ] Next steps include actions, owners, and deadlines
- [ ] Length within 1-3 pages (500-1200 words)
- [ ] Scannable in 2-3 minutes (use headers, bullets, tables)
- [ ] Active voice throughout
- [ ] No jargon unexplained for cross-functional readers
- [ ] Subject/title indicates the decision or topic
- [ ] Standalone: reader needs no other document to understand and decide
- [ ] Framework applied correctly (BLUF/Pyramid/SCQA structure visible)

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Analysis Without Recommendation
The brief presents data but never states what to do. The reader finishes informed but undecided.

**Bad:** "Here are three vendor options with their respective features and pricing."
**Good:** "Recommend Segment ($180K/year). It meets 90% of requirements at 30% lower cost than alternatives."

**Fix:** Always end your analysis with an explicit "Recommend [X] because [Y]" statement.

### Mistake 2: Burying the Lead in Background
Two paragraphs of context before the reader learns what the brief is about.

**Bad:** "As you know, our company has been growing rapidly over the past several quarters. In light of recent market changes and competitive dynamics, our team conducted an evaluation of..."
**Good:** "BLUF: Approve $280K for AI-powered support automation by November 15 to cut support costs 35%."

**Fix:** Write the BLUF first, then decide how much context is truly necessary. Most executives need less background than you think.

### Mistake 3: Qualitative Claims Without Evidence
Using adjectives where numbers belong.

**Bad:** "This will significantly improve our customer experience and drive substantial revenue growth."
**Good:** "Expected outcomes: NPS +26 points (32 to 58), revenue +$2.1M annually from cross-sell targeting."

**Fix:** For every adjective (significant, substantial, improved, better), ask: "What is the number?"

### Mistake 4: Missing the "Do Nothing" Option
Presenting only the recommended option makes the brief look like advocacy rather than analysis.

**Bad:** "We should invest $400K in retention programs."
**Good:** "Option C (Do Nothing): Continue losing $2.4M/year in replacement costs. Attrition likely increases to 35% as competitors expand remote-work offerings."

**Fix:** Always include a "Do Nothing" row in your options comparison. Quantify the cost of inaction.

### Mistake 5: Vague Next Steps
Ending with unclear actions that no one can execute.

**Bad:** "We should move forward with implementation in the near future."
**Good:** "1. Finance approves PO by March 1. 2. Engineering begins integration March 3. 3. First unified profiles available April 1."

**Fix:** Every next step needs three elements: action, owner, and date.

## Length Management

**Target:** 500-1200 words (1-3 pages)

**If exceeding 3 pages:**
- Move detailed financial models to an appendix
- Replace prose with tables and bullet points
- Cut background the audience already knows
- Check if this should be a report instead

**If under 1 page:**
- Verify all options have been analyzed
- Check that evidence is specific (not just assertions)
- Confirm financial analysis is included
- Ensure next steps are complete with owners and dates
