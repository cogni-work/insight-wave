# Trend Scan: Manufacturing Digitalization

## Horizon 1 — Now (0-12 months)

| Dimension | Trend | Signal Strength |
|---|---|---|
| Technology | OPC UA over TSN as factory-floor communication standard | Strong |
| Technology | Edge AI for real-time quality inspection | Strong |
| Market | Retrofit-as-a-Service offerings from machine builders | Emerging |
| Regulation | NIS2 compliance deadline forcing OT security upgrades | Strong |

## Horizon 2 — Next (12-36 months)

| Dimension | Trend | Signal Strength |
|---|---|---|
| Technology | Digital Twin for entire production lines (not just machines) | Moderate |
| Society | Worker augmentation (AR-guided maintenance) replacing worker replacement narrative | Moderate |
| Market | Consolidation of MES vendors — SAP, Siemens, Rockwell acquiring specialists | Strong |
| Economy | EU Green Deal Industrial Plan driving energy-efficiency digitalization | Moderate |

## Horizon 3 — Later (36+ months)

| Dimension | Trend | Signal Strength |
|---|---|---|
| Technology | Autonomous production cells with self-optimizing parameters | Weak |
| Society | Gen Z workforce expects digital-native factory environments | Moderate |
| Market | Manufacturing-as-a-Service platforms enabling distributed production | Weak |
| Regulation | EU AI Act implications for autonomous production decisions | Emerging |

## Cross-Cutting Observations

1. **Retrofit beats greenfield**: Most mid-market investments go to upgrading existing lines, not building new ones
2. **Cybersecurity is the new bottleneck**: OT/IT convergence creates attack surfaces that delay projects 3-6 months
3. **Data > Dashboards**: Companies that use production data for automated decisions see 3x ROI vs. dashboard-only implementations
