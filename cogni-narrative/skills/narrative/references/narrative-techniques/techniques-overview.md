# Narrative Techniques Overview

## Core Techniques

These techniques are applied during narrative transformation to convert structured content into compelling executive narratives.

---

## 1. Pyramid Principle (McKinsey)

**Purpose:** Answer First architecture. Executives get the key insight immediately, then drill into evidence.

**Structure:**
```
LEVEL 1 (Executive): Answer + Recommendations (600 words)
    ↓ [Got answer? STOP]
LEVEL 2 (Context): Scope & Methodology (250 words)
    ↓ [Need evidence? CONTINUE]
LEVEL 3 (Evidence): Patterns + Landscape (500-800 words)
    ↓ [Need detail? DRILL DOWN]
LEVEL 4 (Technical): Appendix + Source links
```

**Application:** Structure the overall narrative so the most important conclusion comes first.

---

## 2. PSB (Problem-Solution-Benefit)

**Purpose:** Reframe findings as unconsidered needs.

**Structure:**
- **Problem (150 words):** Current assumption/status quo and why it's incomplete
- **Solution (150 words):** Unconsidered reality revealed by evidence
- **Benefit (100-200 words):** Competitive advantage for early recognizers

**Key Techniques:**
- Contrast structure: "Most organizations think X. But evidence shows Y."
- Evidence-based reframing with citations
- End with competitive implication

---

## 3. IS-DOES-MEANS (Power Positions)

**Purpose:** Convert recommendations into capabilities that create competitive advantage.

**Structure:**
- **IS (What it is):** Specific, concrete definition (1-2 sentences)
- **DOES (What it does for you):** Quantified outcomes with You-Phrasing
- **MEANS (Why competitors struggle):** Explain the moat/differentiation

**Key Techniques:**
- You-Phrasing throughout DOES layer ("You reduce...", "Your systems...")
- Quantify DOES layer with Number Plays
- Explain competitive moat in MEANS (time, tacit knowledge, experience)

---

## 4. Number Plays (6 Techniques)

**Purpose:** Transform raw data into memorable, impactful quantified claims.

### 4.1 Ratio Framing
"1 in 8 projects fail" instead of "12% failure rate"

### 4.2 Specific Quantification
"127K saved per quarter" instead of "significant cost savings"

### 4.3 Comparative Anchoring
"12 full weeks recovered -- a quarter back" instead of "480 hours/year"

### 4.4 Before/After Contrast
"5 days → 4 hours (97% faster)"

### 4.5 Compound Impact
"10 hrs/week x 52 weeks x 5 people = 130K recovered"

### 4.6 Hero Number Isolation
ONE stat dominates: 36pt bold. Supporting numbers in context text.

---

## 5. Forcing Functions

**Purpose:** Establish urgency through external pressures and deadlines.

**Structure:** Stack 2-3 forcing functions:
- **External pressure:** Regulation, market shift, competitor action
- **Quantified consequence:** Specific cost/penalty with timeline
- **Window of opportunity:** Before/after contrast for early vs. late movers

**Key Techniques:**
- Specific timelines ("Q2 2027" not "soon")
- Quantified consequences ("420K penalties" not "financial risk")
- Before/after contrasts using confidence scores

---

## 6. Contrast Structure

**Purpose:** Create cognitive dissonance that demands resolution.

**Pattern:**
```
"Most [audience] view [topic] as [conventional wisdom].
This framing misses [unconsidered reality].
[Evidence] shows [surprising finding]."
```

**Variants:**
- **Inverse correlation:** "More X leads to less Y" (counterintuitive)
- **Hidden variable:** "X doesn't cause Y; Z causes both"
- **Category error:** "This isn't a [Category A] problem; it's [Category B]"
- **Optimization paradox:** "Optimizing for X reduces Y, which matters more"

---

## 7. You-Phrasing

**Purpose:** Make the narrative feel personally relevant to the reader.

**Transform:**
- "Organizations can reduce..." → "You reduce..."
- "The system provides..." → "Your teams gain..."
- "Companies should invest..." → "Your investment yields..."

**Where to apply:**
- DOES layer of IS-DOES-MEANS
- Benefit sections of PSB
- Strategic recommendations
- Call-to-action elements

---

## 8. Compound Impact Calculation

**Purpose:** Quantify cost of inaction by stacking multiple cost dimensions.

**Formula:**
```
Total Cost of Inaction =
  Regulatory Penalties +
  Talent Premium +
  Market Position Loss +
  Opportunity Cost
```

**Key Techniques:**
- Stack 3-4 cost dimensions
- Use 3-year horizon (standard executive planning)
- Before/after contrast: "Delay costs X vs. Action costs Y"
- End with simple ratio: "Action costs less than inaction by 2-3x"
