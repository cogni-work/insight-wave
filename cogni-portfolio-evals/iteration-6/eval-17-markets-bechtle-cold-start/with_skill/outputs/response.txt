## Strategic Hypothesis: Likely Market Architecture for Bechtle AG

That said, here is where I'd expect the market portfolio to land once features are in place. This is a hypothesis, not a recommendation — the actual market set must be grounded in defined features.

Bechtle is a EUR 6B+ German IT services and system house with ~15,000 employees. They operate as a reseller, managed services provider, and IT consulting firm primarily in DACH and broader Europe. Their business model is channel-heavy: direct sales to mid-market and enterprise, plus e-commerce for IT procurement.

| Region | Rationale |

### Likely Market Segments (once features exist)

| Hypothetical Market | Region | Segment | Why |
|---------------------|--------|---------|-----|
| Mittelstand IT Modernization (DACH) | `dach` | 250-2,500 employees, EUR 50M-500M revenue, manufacturing and industrial verticals | Bechtle's core: German Mittelstand companies modernizing IT infrastructure. High density of system house relationships. |
| Enterprise Managed Services (DACH) | `dach` | 2,500-50,000 employees, EUR 500M-10B revenue, cross-vertical | Large enterprises outsourcing IT operations. Bechtle's managed services and multi-vendor management play here. |
| Public Sector IT (DACH) | `dach` | Government agencies, education, healthcare institutions | Bechtle has dedicated public sector units with framework contracts. Highly defensible, procurement-driven. |
| Mid-Market Cloud Migration (EU) | `eu` | 250-2,500 employees, EUR 50M-500M revenue, cross-vertical | Expansion play: replicating the DACH Mittelstand model in acquired European markets. |

### Key Observations

1. **Beachhead is obvious**: Mittelstand DACH. This is where Bechtle has the deepest moat — 70+ locations in Germany, established procurement relationships, and a system house model that mid-market buyers trust.

2. **Public sector is a distinct market, not a vertical filter**: Public sector procurement in DACH operates on framework agreements with entirely different buying processes. It needs its own market entity, not a vertical tag on a generic segment.

3. **The EU expansion markets are secondary until DACH features are proven**: Bechtle's European acquisitions (Inmac Wstore in France, ARP in Switzerland) operate somewhat independently. Market definition here should follow, not lead, DACH market validation.

4. **Biggest risk**: Defining markets too broadly as "IT services for mid-market" — Bechtle does everything from hardware procurement to cloud consulting. Without features to anchor market definitions, every segment looks addressable, which means none are actionable.
