# Documentation Brief: cogni-research user documentation

This file captures the context, audience, and decisions for creating user-facing documentation about the Deeper Research methodology. Use this as a reference when creating new versions or related materials.

---

## Target Audience

**Primary:** B2B marketing managers at ICT service providers, strategy consulting managers

**Characteristics:**
- Basic AI knowledge, no technical background
- Will NOT use the plugin themselves
- Receive finished deliverables (branded docs, web content, chat interface)
- Need to trust and confidently publish/share the content
- Need to arm sales teams with credible materials

**Secondary audiences:**
- Expert network members (industry experts, customer advisors) who participate in validation
- Evaluators assessing whether the methodology fits their needs
- New stakeholders being onboarded to the process

---

## Document Purpose

**Not:** How to use the tool (readers don't use it)

**Instead:** Why you can trust research created with this methodology

The document serves as:
1. Trust & methodology explainer for content recipients
2. Onboarding material for new stakeholders
3. Evaluation guide for prospects
4. Accompaniment to delivered research content

---

## Core Message

"You can trust this research because every claim is verified, traceable, and human-reviewed."

---

## Key Pain Points to Address

1. Generic AI content looks like everyone else's
2. Customers ask "where did you get that data?" and there's no answer
3. Sales teams hesitate to share content they can't defend
4. One wrong statistic can derail a customer conversation
5. AI hallucination risk undermines credibility

---

## Use Cases for the Research

What readers will do with Deeper Research output:
- Trend reports / market analyses for customer conversations
- Whitepapers to generate leads
- Sales enablement content
- Website thought leadership

---

## The Ecosystem (Who Does What)

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Industry experts | Domain knowledge, accuracy validation |
| Customer advisors | Market relevance validation |
| Methodology specialist (you) | AI orchestration, process rigor |
| Marketing manager (reader) | Receives and publishes finished content |
| Sales team | Uses content and chat interface for customer conversations |

---

## Tone & Style Guidelines

- Professional but accessible
- No technical jargon (explain or remove terms like "RAG")
- Outcome-focused, not process-focused
- Confident without being salesy
- ~1,500 words, 5-7 minute read
- Use analogies (journalism, academic papers, consulting)

---

## Required Sections

1. **Opening hook** - Problem statement about AI content credibility
2. **What is Deeper Research** - Simple definition using analogies
3. **Why it matters for B2B marketing** - Pain points and stakes
4. **How it works** - Simplified process (foundation/structure/finishing)
5. **Trust architecture** - The verification layers explained plainly
6. **Human-in-the-loop** - Expert and customer validation
7. **What you receive** - Deliverable formats
8. **Implementation Details** - Methodology and technology overview
9. **FAQ** - Common marketing manager questions

---

## Key Concepts to Explain Simply

| Technical Concept | Plain Language |
|-------------------|----------------|
| Provenance tracking | "Every claim traces back to its source" |
| Anti-hallucination | "The system is designed to make fabrication structurally difficult" |
| Confidence scoring | "Claims are scored based on how many sources support them, how authoritative those sources are, and how recent the information is" |
| Research dimensions | "Questions are broken into angles like technology trends, market dynamics, regulatory landscape" |
| RAG interface | "A chat interface where your team can ask questions about trends and get sourced answers" |
| Entity pipeline | Don't mention—too technical |

---

## Differentiators to Emphasize

1. **vs. ChatGPT:** Traceable sources, verification layers, human review
2. **vs. Research agencies:** Speed + source-level audit trails
3. **vs. Generic AI content:** Defensible claims, expert validation, customer involvement

---

## Questions the Document Should Answer

- What is this?
- How does it work?
- What does it mean for me?
- Why should I trust it?
- How is it different from ChatGPT?
- Who validates the research?
- What do I receive?
- Can my sales team use it?
- What if a customer challenges a claim?

---

## Reader Testing Observations

When tested with a fresh reader (no context), these issues were identified and fixed:

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| "RAG-powered" unexplained | Replace with "chat interface...sourced answers instantly" |
| "Research dimensions" abstract | Add examples: "technology trends, market dynamics, regulatory landscape" |
| Confidence scores unclear | Explain factors: source count, authority, recency |
| "Your customers" ambiguous | Clarify: "Customer advisors from your target market" |

**Questions readers still had (may address in future versions):**
- Pricing and engagement model
- Specific timelines
- Source types used
- Level of client involvement required
- Expert credentials
- Sample deliverables
