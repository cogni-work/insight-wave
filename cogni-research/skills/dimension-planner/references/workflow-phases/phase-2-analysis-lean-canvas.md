## Embedded Framework Definition

**Source:** Ash Maurya's Lean Canvas (adapted from Business Model Canvas)

This phase file contains all framework content pre-compiled. No runtime loading of external files required.

### Nine Fixed Canvas Blocks

Ash Maurya's Lean Canvas consists of 9 blocks designed to validate business models systematically:

1. **Problem** - Top customer pain points
2. **Customer Segments** - Target markets and early adopters
3. **Unique Value Proposition** - Compelling differentiation
4. **Solution** - Top features addressing problems
5. **Channels** - Paths to customers
6. **Revenue Streams** - Monetization approach
7. **Cost Structure** - Fixed and variable costs
8. **Key Metrics** - Success indicators
9. **Unfair Advantage** - Sustainable competitive moats

### Business Model Validation Flow

```text
Problem → Customer Segments → Unique Value Proposition → Solution →
Channels → Revenue Streams → Cost Structure → Key Metrics → Unfair Advantage
```

**Core Principle:** Each canvas block represents a critical assumption requiring evidence-based validation.

### MECE Validation

The nine blocks are pre-validated for MECE compliance:

**Mutually Exclusive:**

- Problem space (Problem, Customer Segments)
- Solution space (Solution, Unique Value Proposition)
- Go-to-market (Channels, Unfair Advantage)
- Financial (Revenue Streams, Cost Structure, Key Metrics)

**Collectively Exhaustive:**

- Covers all aspects of business model validation
- Maps 1:1 to standard Lean Canvas structure

## Step 1: Apply Embedded Canvas Block Definitions

Use the following pre-compiled block definitions (no external file loading):

### Block 1: Problem

**Slug:** `problem`

**Focus:** Top customer pain points requiring validation

**Key Questions:**

- What are the top 3 problems?
- How painful are they?
- What existing alternatives exist?

**PICOT Pattern:**

- **P:** Target customer segment
- **I:** Problem identification/validation methods
- **C:** Current alternatives/workarounds
- **O:** Problem severity metrics, frequency indicators
- **T:** Validation timeframe

**Evidence Types:** Customer interviews, problem validation research, user behavior data

**Search Keywords:** customer problems, pain points, unmet needs, {segment} challenges, existing alternatives

---

### Block 2: Customer Segments

**Slug:** `customer-segments`

**Focus:** Target markets and early adopter identification

**Key Questions:**

- Who are the target customers?
- Who are the early adopters?
- What are their characteristics?

**PICOT Pattern:**

- **P:** Potential customer segments (broad categories)
- **I:** Segmentation criteria (demographics, behavior, needs)
- **C:** Alternative segment definitions
- **O:** Segment attractiveness (size, growth, accessibility)
- **T:** Market analysis timeframe

**Evidence Types:** Market segmentation studies, TAM/SAM/SOM analysis, demographic data, early adopter research

**Search Keywords:** market segmentation, customer segments, early adopters, TAM SAM SOM, target market

---

### Block 3: Unique Value Proposition

**Slug:** `unique-value-proposition`

**Focus:** Compelling differentiation statement

**Key Questions:**

- What is the single, clear message?
- Why is this different and worth attention?
- What is the high-level concept?

**PICOT Pattern:**

- **P:** Target customer segment
- **I:** Value proposition/unique benefit
- **C:** Competitor value propositions
- **O:** Customer perception/preference
- **T:** Market assessment timeframe

**Evidence Types:** Value proposition case studies, positioning analysis, concept testing, competitor analysis

**Search Keywords:** value proposition, competitive positioning, differentiation, unique selling point, customer value

---

### Block 4: Solution

**Slug:** `solution`

**Focus:** Top features addressing identified problems

**Key Questions:**

- What are the top 3 features?
- How do they map to problems?
- What is the MVP scope?

**PICOT Pattern:**

- **P:** Target customer segment
- **I:** Proposed solution features/capabilities
- **C:** Competitor solutions/alternatives
- **O:** Problem resolution effectiveness, user satisfaction
- **T:** Solution validation timeframe

**Evidence Types:** Product comparisons, feature effectiveness studies, user testing, MVP results

**Search Keywords:** solution features, MVP, product validation, feature effectiveness, user testing

---

### Block 5: Channels

**Slug:** `channels`

**Focus:** Paths to reach customers

**Key Questions:**

- How will customers find you?
- What are the inbound vs outbound channels?
- What is the customer journey?

**PICOT Pattern:**

- **P:** Target customer segment
- **I:** Acquisition channel/distribution method
- **C:** Alternative channels/traditional approaches
- **O:** Acquisition metrics (CAC, conversion rates, reach)
- **T:** Channel performance evaluation period

**Evidence Types:** Channel performance benchmarks, CAC analysis, customer journey mapping, GTM case studies

**Search Keywords:** customer acquisition, distribution channels, CAC benchmarks, go-to-market, channel effectiveness

---

### Block 6: Revenue Streams

**Slug:** `revenue-streams`

**Focus:** Monetization approach and pricing

**Key Questions:**

- What is the revenue model?
- What is the pricing strategy?
- What is the lifetime value?

**PICOT Pattern:**

- **P:** Target customer segment
- **I:** Pricing model/revenue approach
- **C:** Competitor pricing/alternative models
- **O:** Revenue metrics (ARPU, LTV, payment conversion)
- **T:** Pricing analysis timeframe

**Evidence Types:** Pricing strategy research, willingness-to-pay studies, SaaS metrics (ARPU, LTV), financial reports

**Search Keywords:** pricing strategy, revenue model, willingness to pay, monetization, ARPU LTV

---

### Block 7: Cost Structure

**Slug:** `cost-structure`

**Focus:** Fixed and variable costs

**Key Questions:**

- What are the fixed costs?
- What are the variable costs?
- What is the burn rate?

**PICOT Pattern:**

- **P:** Business model/company stage
- **I:** Cost category/optimization approach
- **C:** Industry benchmarks/alternative cost structures
- **O:** Economic metrics (gross margin, burn rate, cost per unit)
- **T:** Financial analysis timeframe

**Evidence Types:** Cost structure analysis, gross margin benchmarks, operational cost studies, financial reports

**Search Keywords:** cost structure, fixed costs, variable costs, gross margin, burn rate, cost optimization

---

### Block 8: Key Metrics

**Slug:** `key-metrics`

**Focus:** Success indicators and KPIs

**Key Questions:**

- What are the key activities to measure?
- What are the leading indicators?
- What defines success?

**PICOT Pattern:**

- **P:** Business model/industry segment
- **I:** Key metric/KPI category
- **C:** Alternative metrics/measurement approaches
- **O:** Business outcomes (growth, retention, profitability)
- **T:** Metric tracking timeframe

**Evidence Types:** KPI framework research, industry benchmark reports, metric correlation studies

**Search Keywords:** key metrics, KPI benchmarks, {industry} metrics, performance indicators, success metrics

---

### Block 9: Unfair Advantage

**Slug:** `unfair-advantage`

**Focus:** Sustainable competitive moats

**Key Questions:**

- What cannot be easily copied or bought?
- What are the barriers to entry?
- What network effects exist?

**PICOT Pattern:**

- **P:** Company/business model type
- **I:** Competitive advantage/moat type OR Competitor analysis approach
- **C:** Competitors/market entrants
- **O:** Defensibility metrics (barriers, switching costs, network effects) OR Competitive positioning
- **T:** Competitive analysis timeframe

**Evidence Types:** Competitive strategy research, moat analysis, barriers to entry studies, market share analysis

**Search Keywords:** unfair advantage, competitive moat, barriers to entry, market share {industry}, competitive landscape

**Note:** Unfair Advantage dimension should generate 5-6 questions covering: competitive landscape mapping, competitor profiling, positioning analysis, moat assessment, and strategic response scenarios.

## Step 2: Detect Business Stage from Question

Analyze the question for stage indicators using `<extended_thinking>`:

**Pre-launch stage:**

- Keywords: "validate", "test assumptions", "problem-solution fit", "customer discovery"
- Focus weighting: Problem (HIGH), Customer Segments (HIGH), Solution (HIGH)
- Secondary: UVP (MEDIUM), Channels (LOW), Revenue (LOW)

**Product-market fit stage:**

- Keywords: "product-market fit", "scaling validation", "customer acquisition"
- Focus weighting: UVP (HIGH), Channels (HIGH), Revenue Streams (MEDIUM)
- Secondary: Solution (MEDIUM), Key Metrics (MEDIUM), Unfair Advantage (LOW)

**Scaling stage:**

- Keywords: "scale", "growth", "expand", "optimize"
- Focus weighting: Channels (HIGH), Revenue Streams (HIGH), Key Metrics (HIGH)
- Secondary: Cost Structure (MEDIUM), Unfair Advantage (HIGH)

**Default (no stage detected):**

- Equal weighting across all 9 blocks

## Step 3: Apply Stage-Specific Focus Weighting

```bash
# Set focus weighting based on detected stage
case "$BUSINESS_STAGE" in
  pre-launch)
    FOCUS_BLOCKS="problem customer-segments solution"
    FOCUS_WEIGHT_HIGH=6  # questions per high-priority block
    FOCUS_WEIGHT_MEDIUM=4  # questions per medium-priority block
    FOCUS_WEIGHT_LOW=3  # questions per low-priority block
    ;;
  pmf)
    FOCUS_BLOCKS="unique-value-proposition channels revenue-streams"
    FOCUS_WEIGHT_HIGH=6
    FOCUS_WEIGHT_MEDIUM=4
    FOCUS_WEIGHT_LOW=3
    ;;
  scaling)
    FOCUS_BLOCKS="channels revenue-streams key-metrics unfair-advantage"
    FOCUS_WEIGHT_HIGH=6
    FOCUS_WEIGHT_MEDIUM=4
    FOCUS_WEIGHT_LOW=3
    ;;
  *)
    FOCUS_BLOCKS=""  # No stage-specific focus
    FOCUS_WEIGHT_HIGH=5  # Equal weighting
    FOCUS_WEIGHT_MEDIUM=5
    FOCUS_WEIGHT_LOW=5
    ;;
esac

log_conditional INFO "[lean-canvas] Focus blocks: ${FOCUS_BLOCKS:-all blocks equally weighted}"
```
