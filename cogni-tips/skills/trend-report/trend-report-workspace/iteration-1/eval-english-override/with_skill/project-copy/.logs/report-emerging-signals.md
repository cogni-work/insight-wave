## Emerging Signals

The following candidates were not assigned to any strategic theme. They represent early signals worth monitoring -- their absence from current themes may itself be informative.

### Water Scarcity & Grid Operations (T)

Climate-driven water scarcity threatens cooling water for thermal power plants and data centers through 2032. While not yet linked to a strategic theme, this observe-horizon signal could rapidly escalate if extreme weather events accelerate, potentially requiring a dedicated climate adaptation theme.

---

### Procurement Threshold Adjustments (T)

Updated EU public procurement thresholds for 2026-2027 create new compliance requirements for municipal utilities. This regulatory housekeeping signal affects procurement processes but does not currently drive strategic investment decisions.

---

### Grid Resilience Post-Extreme Weather (T)

Increasing extreme weather frequency forces resilience investments in grid redundancy and recovery speed. This observe-horizon signal intersects with Theme 1 (Grid Optimization) but was not formally linked to a value chain. As climate events intensify, this may warrant elevation to a theme-level concern.

---

### Carbon Pricing Expansion (T)

ETS expansion to heating and transport by 2028 transforms energy cost structures. While related to Theme 5 (Decarbonization), this was not directly assigned to a value chain. The cost impact across all tariff structures warrants close monitoring.

---

### Engie UK Power Networks M&A (P)

The GBP 10.5 billion Engie acquisition signals European network operator consolidation. This act-horizon orphan may indicate a gap in the theme model: M&A-driven transformation is not captured by any current theme but could reshape the competitive landscape.

---

### Klir Series B Data Hub (P)

Klir's EUR 17.5 million Series B for utility data hub solutions validates market demand for specialized operational data platforms. This startup ecosystem signal represents the innovation frontier that larger utilities must monitor and potentially partner with.

---

### Agile Operating Models (P)

The transition from centralized to decentralized, agile team structures by 2032 is an organizational transformation signal. While cultural change appears in Theme 2 (as a foundation requirement), the full operating model transformation is not captured by any theme's value chains.
