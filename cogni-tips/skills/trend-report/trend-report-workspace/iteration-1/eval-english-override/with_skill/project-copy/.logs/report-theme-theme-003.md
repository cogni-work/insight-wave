## 3. Digital Customer Experience & New Revenue Models

> How do we transform customer experience from 616/1000 to best-in-class and enable new digital revenue streams through energy sharing and prosumer platforms?

**Executive Sponsor:** CCO / VP Customer Experience

### Investment Thesis

The 2026 J.D. Power Utility Digital Experience Study reveals a sector-wide CX deficit: overall satisfaction stands at just 616 on a 1,000-point scale, with a 100-point gap separating leaders (660) from laggards (560) [J.D. Power](https://www.jdpower.com/business/press-releases/2026-us-utility-digital-experience-study). Meanwhile, the Electricity Market Reform 2026 and Energy Sharing Regulation effective July 2026 are reshaping customer relationships by enabling direct contracts and peer-to-peer energy trading. 83% of customers demand real-time outage notifications [Methodia](https://www.methodia.com/blog/customer-experience-trends-in-utilities), yet 28% of utilities still lack mobile apps -- despite apps scoring 63 points higher than mobile websites. The competitive landscape is clear: utilities that deliver consumer-grade digital experiences will capture the emerging prosumer market, while those stuck in legacy customer interaction models will face accelerating churn in liberalized markets. This theme encompasses the full customer transformation: 360-degree data strategies for personalization, omnichannel support for seamless service, mobile-first payment platforms for frictionless transactions, and platform capabilities for P2P energy trading and fintech partnerships.

### Value Chains

#### Market Reform to Customer Transformation

**Trend:** Electricity Market Reform 2026
Reformed electricity market design is reducing volatility through decentralization and promoting direct contracts between generators and consumers, enabling new tariff structures and PPA mechanisms [ING](https://think.ing.com/articles/energy-european-utilities-2026-steady-currents/).

**Implication:**
- **Digital Customer Experience 616/1000** -- The J.D. Power 616/1000 score reveals massive optimization potential. Mobile apps score 654 on average -- 63 points higher than mobile websites (591) -- yet 28% of utilities still lack mobile apps.
- **Customer Data Strategy 360 Degrees** -- Unified consumption profiles enable tariff optimization, energy saving recommendations, and upselling based on comprehensive customer understanding.

**Possibility:**
- **Cross-Sector Energy Trading** -- B2B platforms for electricity trading between generators, consumers, and aggregators are emerging through smart contract technology, creating new market intermediation opportunities.

**Foundation Requirements:**
- **Unified Data Platforms** -- IT/OT data convergence enables the single customer view that personalization and CX optimization require.

---

#### Energy Sharing to Prosumer Platforms

**Trend:** Energy Sharing Regulation
By July 2026, new EU rules enable multiple suppliers per premises and incentivize energy sharing through interoperable data exchange [European Commission](https://energy.ec.europa.eu/topics/markets-and-consumers/energy-consumers-and-prosumers/protecting-and-empowering-energy-consumers_en).

**Implication:**
- **Personalized Energy Saving Tips** -- AI-based recommendations reduce consumption by 8-12% while strengthening customer loyalty, creating value from the data infrastructure that energy sharing requires.
- **Real-Time Outage Notifications** -- 83% of customers demand SMS/push notifications. Proactive communication reduces inbound call volume by 30-40% during events and improves satisfaction by 15-20 points.

**Possibility:**
- **P2P Energy Trading Blockchain** -- Peer-to-peer trading between prosumers normalizing by 2028-2032 through blockchain and smart contracts, with regulatory frameworks launching July 2026.
- **Utility-Fintech Partnerships** -- Payment innovation enables subscription models, micropayments for EV charging, and integrated financial products that deepen customer relationships.

**Foundation Requirements:**
- **API-Based Legacy Modernization** -- API gateways enable new digital customer experiences while preserving proven backend billing and metering processes.

---

#### Churn Reduction to Customer Retention

**Trend:** Electricity Market Reform 2026
Market liberalization intensifies competitive pressure, making customer retention a strategic priority as switching costs decrease.

**Implication:**
- **Predictive Churn Models** -- ML-based churn prediction identifies 90-day attrition risks, enabling targeted retention interventions that reduce defection by 15-25%.
- **Omnichannel Customer Support** -- Seamless support across chat, phone, app, and web with context continuity reduces handling time and improves first-contact resolution.
- **Mobile-First Payment Platforms** -- Digital wallet integration reduces payment defaults and support costs by approximately 20% while meeting digital-native customer expectations.
- **Service as Loyalty Driver** -- Customer service quality is becoming the primary loyalty driver, surpassing price as the key retention factor in liberalized markets.

**Possibility:**
- **Utility-Fintech Partnerships** -- Payment innovation and subscription models create switching barriers through embedded financial services.

**Foundation Requirements:**
- **Cloud Migration & Data Lakehouse** -- Cloud infrastructure provides the compute and analytics capabilities needed for churn prediction and personalization at scale.

### Strategic Actions

1. **Launch utility mobile app with bill pay, outage tracking, and usage analytics** -- Close the 28% mobile app gap and capture the 63-point satisfaction premium that mobile apps deliver over mobile websites.
2. **Deploy real-time outage notification infrastructure** -- Meet the 83% customer demand for SMS/push outage alerts, reducing call center load by 30-40% during events.
3. **Build prosumer platform capabilities for July 2026 energy sharing deadline** -- Develop the platform infrastructure that positions the utility as the default operator for decentralized energy communities.
4. **Implement predictive churn analytics** -- Deploy ML models that identify at-risk customers 90 days in advance, enabling targeted retention that reduces defection by 15-25%.
5. **Integrate digital wallet payments** -- Add Apple Pay, Google Pay support to reduce payment friction, lower default rates, and meet digital-native customer expectations.
