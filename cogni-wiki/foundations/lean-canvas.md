# Lean Canvas

One-page business-model template adapted by Ash Maurya from Alexander
Osterwalder's Business Model Canvas, optimised for early-stage startups.
Nine blocks capture the riskiest assumptions on a single sheet:

1. **Problem** — top-3 problems the customer has today.
2. **Customer Segments** — early adopters first, mass market later.
3. **Unique Value Proposition** — the single, clear, compelling promise.
4. **Solution** — the smallest set of features that addresses the problems.
5. **Channels** — how the product reaches customers.
6. **Revenue Streams** — how the business makes money.
7. **Cost Structure** — fixed and variable costs.
8. **Key Metrics** — the few numbers that prove the model is working.
9. **Unfair Advantage** — the moat that competitors can't copy or buy.

When to reach for it: early product or venture work where the team is
debating features but has not pressure-tested the underlying business
hypothesis. The canvas is not a plan; it is a list of assumptions in rank
order to test next.
