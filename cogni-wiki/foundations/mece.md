# MECE

"Mutually Exclusive, Collectively Exhaustive" — a structuring rule popular
at McKinsey for decomposing a problem into sub-problems. A breakdown is
MECE when:

- **Mutually Exclusive** — every observation falls into exactly one
  category; categories do not overlap.
- **Collectively Exhaustive** — every observation falls into at least one
  category; nothing is missed.

The two halves are usually traded off: the more orthogonal you make
categories (ME), the harder it gets to keep them comprehensive (CE), and
vice versa. The MECE check is whether a list passes both filters at once.

When to reach for it: any time a recommendation or analysis depends on a
breakdown — segments, root causes, options, drivers. A non-MECE breakdown
double-counts or omits, both of which cost credibility downstream.
