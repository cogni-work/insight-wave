# OODA Loop

Decision cycle developed by USAF Colonel John Boyd from fighter-combat
analysis. An agent in a contested environment moves through four phases
on a continuous loop:

1. **Observe** — gather raw data from the environment.
2. **Orient** — interpret data through mental models, experience, and
   cultural context. Boyd considered this the most decisive phase.
3. **Decide** — pick a course of action.
4. **Act** — execute, then observe the result and re-enter the loop.

The competitive insight: an agent who runs the loop faster than an
opponent — especially in the Orient phase — operates inside the
opponent's decision cycle and forces them to react to stale data.

When to reach for it: fast-moving competitive contexts (markets, crisis
response, negotiations) where speed of correct re-orientation matters more
than the elegance of any single decision.
