# Porter's Five Forces

Industry-structure framework introduced by Michael Porter (HBR, 1979). The
profitability of a firm is shaped by five forces operating at the level of
its industry, not its competitors:

1. **Threat of new entrants** — barriers to entry, capital intensity,
   regulation.
2. **Bargaining power of suppliers** — concentration, switching cost, unique
   inputs.
3. **Bargaining power of buyers** — concentration, price sensitivity,
   substitution availability.
4. **Threat of substitutes** — adjacent products solving the same job.
5. **Rivalry among existing competitors** — growth rate, exit barriers,
   product differentiation.

When to reach for it: industry-level diagnosis at the start of a strategy
engagement, sanity-check on whether a market is structurally attractive
before pricing or positioning work.
