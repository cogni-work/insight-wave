# Porter's Value Chain

Activity-level decomposition introduced by Michael Porter (Competitive
Advantage, 1985). A firm is modelled as a sequence of activities that
each add value; the firm's margin is the sum of value created minus the
cost of every activity.

Two activity classes:

- **Primary** — Inbound logistics, Operations, Outbound logistics,
  Marketing & sales, Service.
- **Support** — Firm infrastructure, Human resource management, Technology
  development, Procurement.

Strategy is the choice of where in the chain to invest for cost leadership
or differentiation, and which activities to outsource. Cross-firm value
chains compose into industry-level value systems.

When to reach for it: cost-position diagnostics, make-vs-buy decisions,
and operating-model design. Pairs with Five Forces — value chain explains
*how* a firm captures the structural rents the industry offers.
