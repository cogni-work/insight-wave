# Wardley Mapping

Strategic mapping technique developed by Simon Wardley. A map plots a
value chain (anchored on a user need) against an evolution axis — how
mature each component is, from genesis through custom-built and product
to commodity / utility.

Two axes:

- **Vertical: Visibility to user.** Top of the map = what the user sees
  directly; bottom = invisible underlying components.
- **Horizontal: Evolution.** Genesis → Custom-built → Product/Rental →
  Commodity/Utility. Components migrate rightward over time.

Strategic moves become legible: invest in genesis components for
differentiation; outsource commodity components; defend product-stage
components against commoditisation pressure.

When to reach for it: technology-strategy or platform-strategy questions
where the team is debating build vs buy across many components, or where
a competitor's commoditisation play threatens a once-defensible
differentiator.
